## Why Smart Locks Are Worth the Investment

Smart locks have moved from novelty to necessity for homeowners who value both **convenience and security**. With features like remote access, auto-locking, guest codes, and activity logs, smart locks offer a level of control that traditional deadbolts simply cannot match.

## Types of Smart Locks

### Keypad Locks

**Keypad smart locks** use numeric codes for entry. They are the simplest smart lock option and do not require a smartphone. Perfect for families with children who might lose keys.

### Bluetooth Locks

These locks connect directly to your smartphone via **Bluetooth**. You can unlock your door as you approach, share virtual keys with guests, and view an access log — all from your phone.

### Wi-Fi Locks

**Wi-Fi-enabled smart locks** connect to your home network, giving you remote access from anywhere in the world. Lock or unlock your door, receive real-time alerts, and integrate with smart home systems like Alexa or Google Home.

## Key Features to Consider

When choosing a smart lock, pay attention to these **critical features**:

### Battery Life

Most smart locks run on standard AA or CR123A batteries. Look for models with **6-12 month battery life** and low-battery alerts so you are never locked out.

### Auto-Lock

An **auto-lock feature** automatically secures your door after a set period, so you never have to worry about forgetting to lock up.

### Compatibility

Make sure the smart lock fits your existing **door prep and deadbolt cutout**. Most standard doors are compatible, but older or non-standard doors may require modifications.

## Professional Installation Matters

While many smart locks are marketed as DIY-friendly, **professional installation** ensures proper alignment, secure mounting, and correct programming. A poorly installed smart lock can be worse than no lock at all — it creates a false sense of security.
